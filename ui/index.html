Architecture & Math

A step-by-step mathematical breakdown of the TinyLLM forward pass.

The complete inference step of the network, from token IDs to output logits, can be summarized mathematically by the following consolidated equations. Below, we break down each mechanism in logical order.

$$ \begin{aligned} H_0 &= \text{Embed}(X) \\ H_l &= F_l(H_{l-1}) \quad \text{for } l \in \{1, 2, \dots, N\} \\ \text{Logits}(X) &= \text{RMSNorm}_{\text{final}}(H_N) W_{\text{head}}^T \\ F_l(H) &= \tilde{H} + \left[ \text{SiLU}\left( \text{RMSNorm}_{\text{ffn}}(\tilde{H}) W_1^T \right) \odot \left( \text{RMSNorm}_{\text{ffn}}(\tilde{H}) W_3^T \right) \right] W_2^T \\ \text{where} \quad \tilde{H} &= H + A_l\big(\text{RMSNorm}_{\text{attn}}(H)\big) \\ A_l(x) &= \text{softmax}\left(\frac{\text{RoPE}(x W_q^T)\text{RoPE}(x W_k^T)^T}{\sqrt{d_{\text{head}}}} + M\right)(x W_v^T) W_o^T \end{aligned} $$

1. Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE Tokenization)

Before any math occurs, raw text must be converted into an array of integers. Instead of splitting by words or characters, BPE optimally compresses text by iteratively merging the most frequent adjacent character pairs found in the training corpus.

2. Rotary Positional Embeddings (RoPE)

Transformers have no inherent sense of order. Unlike absolute positional embeddings that are simply added to the input, RoPE injects positional information by rotating the Query and Key vectors in a complex 2D plane. For a vector pair \((x_1, x_2)\) at position \(m\), the rotation is applied as:

$$ \begin{pmatrix} q_1 \\ q_2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} \cos(m\theta) & -\sin(m\theta) \\ \sin(m\theta) & \cos(m\theta) \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{pmatrix} $$

This allows the attention dot-product to naturally decay as the relative distance between two tokens increases.

3. Multi-Head Self-Attention

Attention is the core mechanism allowing tokens to communicate. For each token, we project it into a Query (\(Q\)). We then compute the dot product between this Query and every previous token's Key (\(K\)) to determine how much "attention" or relevance to assign to it. Finally, we multiply those attention weights by the Values (\(V\)).

$$ \text{Attention}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}\left(\frac{Q K^T}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)V $$

4. Causal Attention Masking

During training, the model processes all tokens simultaneously. To prevent a token from "cheating" by looking into the future, we apply a causal mask (\(M\)) to the upper triangle of the \(Q \times K^T\) attention matrix.

5. SwiGLU Activation Function

After attention, the information is processed by a Feed-Forward Network utilizing a Swish-Gated Linear Unit (SwiGLU). It computes two separate linear projections, applies the SiLU activation \(x \cdot \sigma(x)\) to one, and multiplies them together:

$$ \text{SwiGLU}(x) = \text{SiLU}(x W_1^T) \odot (x W_3^T) $$

This element-wise gating mechanism acts as an information filter, selectively passing relevant features forward.

6. Top-K Sampling & Softmax

At the very end, the model outputs raw logits for every word in the vocabulary. We apply Top-K filtering to eliminate unlikely hallucinated tokens, and then apply Temperature and Softmax to convert the logits into a valid probability distribution (summing to 1.0).

7. KV Caching (Inference Optimization)

During inference, generating the \(N^{\text{th}}\) token requires computing attention over all \(N-1\) previous tokens. To avoid recalculating the Keys and Values for previous tokens, we dynamically cache them in memory:

$$ K_{\text{cache}} = [K_0, K_1, \dots, K_{N-1}] \quad \text{and} \quad V_{\text{cache}} = [V_0, V_1, \dots, V_{N-1}] $$

When the new query \(Q_N\) is generated, it simply attends to the cached matrices, converting a highly expensive \(O(N^2)\) operation into a fast \(O(N)\) operation per generated token.

Technical Implementation

Key components that make TinyLLM efficient and powerful.

  • Rotary Positional Embeddings (RoPE)

    Injects relative positional information directly into the attention mechanism by rotating the query and key representations. It uses a complex frequency spectrum to map positions.

  • Root Mean Square Normalization (RMSNorm)

    Used instead of standard LayerNorm. It removes the mean-centering step for maximum computational efficiency. Applied in a "Pre-Norm" fashion before Attention and FFN blocks.

  • SwiGLU Activation

    The Feed-Forward Network uses the Swish-Gated Linear Unit, utilizing the element-wise Hadamard product to gate information flow intelligently.

  • Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE)

    A custom Python tokenizer that parses raw text and merges adjacent character pairs based on corpus frequency, matching HuggingFace spec.
